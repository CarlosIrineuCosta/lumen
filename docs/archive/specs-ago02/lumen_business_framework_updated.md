## Content Policy Clarifications

### Transparency Approach
Instead of "We don't allow X because we said so," the platform explains:

"Some content is marked NSFW to comply with international laws. This keeps our platform accessible globally. We're not making artistic judgments - we're following rules to stay online for everyone."

### Age Verification
**Steam/RedGifs Model**:
- Simple checkbox declaration
- Cookie-based remembering
- No expensive ID verification
- Legal compliance achieved

### NSFW Handling
**User Control**:
- Show all content (default for paid users)
- Blur NSFW (click to reveal)
- Hide NSFW completely

**AI assists but doesn't dictate** - users can appeal incorrect NSFW tags

## Security Infrastructure Costs

### Often Overlooked Expenses
- **DDoS Protection**: Cloudflare Pro ($200/month at scale)
- **Backup Systems**: ~$50/month for redundancy
- **Monitoring Tools**: ~$100/month for proper alerting
- **Legal Compliance**: ~$500/month retained lawyer

### Total Infrastructure at Scale
- 1,000 users: ~$500/month
- 5,000 users: ~$1,500/month
- 10,000 users: ~$3,000/month

## The Invitation-Only Advantage

### Quality Control During Beta
- Personal vetting of first 200 users
- Word-of-mouth growth only
- No marketing spend needed
- Community self-polices

### Building Reputation
- Each user personally invited
- High engagement expected
- Feedback directly implemented
- Creates exclusive feel without being exclusionary

## Risk Mitigation Updates

### Platform Risks
- **App Store Rejection**: PWA-first approach
- **Payment Processing**: Multiple providers ready
- **Legal Challenges**: Clear terms, good lawyer
- **Competition**: Focus on niche excellence

### Community Risks
- **Bad Actors**: Paid-only reduces significantly

- **Stagnation**: Regular feature updates
- **Drama**: Professional focus reduces this

## The Long Game

### Why This Works
1. **Real Need**: Photographers/models need uncensored portfolios
2. **Clear Differentiator**: No ads, no algorithms, no BS
3. **Sustainable Model**: Subscription > advertising
4. **Quality Focus**: Better to serve 1,000 well than 1M poorly

### When to Raise Prices
- Beta pricing expires after 6 months
- Grandfather early supporters
- New users pay standard rates
- Annual discounts available

### Exit Strategies (Years 3-5)
- Acquisition by photography company
- Merge with larger creative platform
- Open source the technology
- Community ownership model

## Key Decisions for Immediate Future

1. **Payment Provider**: Stripe (supports PIX, SEPA, cards)
2. **Beta User Selection**: Personal network first
3. **Feature Prioritization**: Fix auth, then discovery
4. **Legal Structure**: LLC or similar (protection + flexibility)

The platform succeeds by staying focused on photographers' actual needs, not trying to be everything to everyone. $5-25/month is reasonable for professionals who currently have NO good options for uncensored, algorithm-free portfolio hosting with real networking features.
